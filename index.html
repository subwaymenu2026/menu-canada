Wraps

Wraps sit in the same price range as six-inch subs and come filled with the same proteins and fresh vegetables. They're a solid choice if you prefer a lighter, handheld option over a full footlong.

Power Bowls

Power Bowls start at $10.99 CAD and skip the bread entirely. They're built around protein and vegetables, which makes them popular with people tracking carbs or calories.

Salads

Salads use the same ingredients as the subs but served over greens. Pricing sits close to the six-inch range. If you're trying to cut calories significantly, a salad with a lean protein like turkey and light dressing comes in well under 400 calories.

Kids Meals

Subway designs its kids meals to stay between 350 and 600 calories, making them a reasonable fast food option for younger eaters. Each kids meal includes a mini sub, a side, and a drink.

Sides and Cookies

The most affordable item on the menu is the Chocolate Chip Cookie at C$1.48. Chips and other snack sides sit in a similar range.

Drinks

Fountain drinks, bottled water, and juices are available at most locations. Fountain drinks typically run $2.49 to $3.49 CAD depending on size.

Subway Canada Prices 2026 — What You Can Expect to Pay

Subway menu prices in Canada vary by location, but overall they remain competitive compared to other fast food chains. A six-inch sub is usually the most affordable way to eat, while footlongs, premium proteins, and combos push the total higher. If you're in a major city like Toronto or Vancouver, expect to pay slightly more than you would in a smaller town. That's not unique to Subway. It reflects the higher operating costs in dense urban areas.

Prices can differ between provinces and even between cities based on local operating costs, rent, and taxes. That said, the difference is usually a dollar or two at most. The national price ranges in this guide give you a reliable baseline for planning your order before you arrive.
