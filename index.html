Subway Canada Prices 2026 — What You Can Expect to Pay

Subway menu prices in Canada vary by location, but overall they remain competitive compared to other fast food chains. A six-inch sub is usually the most affordable way to eat, while footlongs, premium proteins, and combos push the total higher. Prices can differ between provinces and even between cities based on local operating costs, rent, and taxes. That said, the difference is usually a dollar or two at most. The national price ranges in this guide give you a reliable baseline for planning your order before you arrive.

Are Subway Canada Limited-Time Items Worth Ordering?

Limited-time offers at Subway Canada follow a pretty consistent pattern. A new item or promotional build drops, gets heavy visibility through the app and in-store signage, runs for six to twelve weeks, and then disappears. Some return the following year. Others don't.

The honest answer is yes, most of them are worth trying at least once. Subway's LTO items tend to use the same quality base ingredients as the core menu, just with a different sauce profile or protein combination. The Bourbon Brisket, for example, started as a limited-time item and became a semi-permanent fixture on the Canadian menu because demand held up. That's the benchmark. If an LTO consistently sells well in Canada, it stays. If not, it rotates out.

The practical advice here is to check the Subway Canada website or app when you're planning a visit. Don't assume the item you want is still available. LTOs can end mid-week without much warning, especially at busy franchise locations that sell through their allocated stock faster than expected.

How to Customize Your Subway Order in Canada

This is where Subway genuinely earns its reputation. The customization options are broad enough that two people can order the same base sub and end up with completely different meals.

Here's what you can control at every step:

  • Bread: Italian white, Italian herbs and cheese, nine-grain wheat, nine-grain honey oat, flatbread, and multigrain are the standard options at most Canadian locations
  • Protein: Chicken, turkey, ham, tuna, steak, meatballs, veggie patty, and rotisserie-style options depending on location
  • Cheese: American, provolone, Swiss, pepper jack, and shredded Monterey cheddar
  • Vegetables: Lettuce, tomato, cucumber, green pepper, red onion, banana peppers, jalapeños, black olives, and pickles
  • Sauces: Ranch, chipotle southwest, honey mustard, sweet onion, mayo, light mayo, mustard, oil, vinegar, and buffalo
  • Toasting: Any sub can be toasted, but some bread types hold up better than others under heat

One thing experienced Subway customers know is to ask for sauce on the side when building anything with a wetter filling like meatball marinara. It keeps the bread from going soft before you finish eating, which matters more with a footlong than a six-inch.

Subway Canada Healthy and Dietary Options

Subway's health positioning has always been one of its stronger selling points, and the 2026 Canadian menu holds up reasonably well when you know what to look for.

Here's what's worth knowing if you're eating with nutrition in mind:

  • The Veggie Delite is the lowest-calorie sub on the menu. A six-inch Veggie Delite comes in at just 220 calories, which makes it one of the lightest fast food options available in Canada
  • Turkey Breast is the leanest protein option. A six-inch turkey sub with vegetables and mustard stays comfortably under 300 calories
  • Vegetarian subs like the Veggie Delite or Veggie Patty typically start around $5.69 to $8.30 CAD for a six-inch, making plant-based eating genuinely affordable here
  • The Veggie Delite can be made vegan by skipping the cheese
  • Gluten-free bread is not currently a standard option across Canada. Salads and Power Bowls are the best choice for anyone needing to avoid gluten entirely
  • For allergen details, Health Canada's food labeling guidelines and the Subway Canada nutrition page are the two most reliable references

The biggest mistake health-conscious customers make is loading up on sauces. Chipotle southwest and ranch are both high in fat and calories. Swapping either for mustard or vinegar cuts the calorie count significantly without sacrificing much flavour.

Tips for Getting the Best Value at Subway Canada

Knowing the menu is one thing. Knowing how to order smart is something else entirely.

Here's what regular Subway Canada customers do differently:

  • Always check the app before ordering. The fastest way to find live deals is through the Subway app, which shows current promo codes and MVP rewards in real time
  • Use the Sub of the Day deal. The daily lineup rotates through different subs each day of the week, and the meal deal comes in at $7.99, which is strong value by any fast food standard in 2026
  • Consider the six-inch over the footlong if you're not very hungry. The price difference is significant, and a six-inch with a cookie and drink as a combo often works out to similar or better value than a standalone footlong
  • Join the Sub Club. Points accumulate faster than most people expect, and the app-exclusive deals are genuinely worth it if you eat at Subway more than once a month
  • Avoid add-ons that inflate the price without adding much. Extra cheese, double meat, and avocado are the three most common items that push a mid-range sub into premium price territory without a proportional jump in satisfaction
